Food Delivery Patterns in Modern Cities

In many cities, delivery services for food like Uber Eats, DoorDash, and Grubhub have grown into a crucial component of daily life. Due to online platforms and mobile apps, the way people order and consume has changed drastically over the past ten years. Nowadays, a lot of people prefer order food directly from their phones compared to visit restaurants. These platforms promote real-time delivery tracking, price comparison, and restaurant browsing. The way that various lifestyles and urban settings appear in food delivery patterns is a fascinating feature. Due to busy schedules and easy access to delivery drivers, people in big cities typically place more frequent food orders. Students are among the most frequent users of delivery apps because they value the convenience of these platforms Another factor influencing food delivery patterns is the type of neighborhood people live in. Areas with many restaurants and dense populations usually have more delivery options and faster delivery times. In our project, we analyze a dataset that contains around 2,600 food delivery orders with several variables describing each order. These include information such as the customer’s age, restaurant type, order value, delivery distance, payment method, and delivery time. By examining this data, we want to understand what factors influence how long deliveries take and how much customers spend when ordering food online. For example, delivery time may be related to distance, restaurant type, or order volume at different times of the week.

Common Food Delivery Trends

  • Restaurant type and average order value
  • Delivery distance and delivery time
  • Payment methods used by customers
  • Order patterns across different days
  • Customer demographics and spending behavior
